\section{Universal formal group laws}

In this section, we discuss the construction of universal formal group laws and their applications.

\begin{definition}[{\cite[Chap.~1, Definition 1.5.2]{hazewinkel1978formal}}]
Let $L$ be a commutative ring.
A formal group law $F(X,Y)$ over $L$ is called a \emph{universal formal group law} if, for any
formal group law $G(X,Y)$ over a commutative ring $R$, there exists a unique ring homomorphism
$\phi : L \to R$ such that $\phi_*F(X,Y) = G(X,Y)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The ring $L$ is uniquely determined up to isomorphism. Indeed,
let $F(X,Y)$ and $G(X,Y)$ be universal formal group laws over rings $L$ and $L'$,
respectively. Then there exist ring homomorphisms $\phi : L \to L'$ and $\psi : L' \to L$
such that $\phi_* F(X,Y) = G(X,Y)$ and $\psi_* G(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$. By uniqueness,
we deduce that $\phi \circ \psi = \id_{L'}$ and $\psi \circ \phi = \id_L$.
In particular, $\phi$ and $\psi$ are isomorphisms.

We now show that there exists a universal formal group law over some ring $L$.
Let $\tilde{L} = \ZZ [C_{i,j}]$, where $C_{i,j}$ are indeterminates with $i,j \in \NN_{>0}$.
Consider the power series $F_C(X,Y) = X + Y + \sum_{i,j \ge 1} C_{i,j} X^i Y^j$. We write

\[F_C(F_C(X,Y),Z) - F_C(X,F_C(Y,Z)) = \sum_{i,j,k \ge 1} P_{i,j,k}(C) X^i Y^j Z^k,\]

where $P_{i,j,k}(C)$ is a polynomial in the variables $C_{i,j}$.
Let $I$ be the ideal of $\tilde{L}$ generated by $C_{i,j} - C_{j,i}$ and $P_{i,j,k}(C)$,
where $i,j,k \in \NN_{>0}$. Define $L = \tilde{L}/I$, and let $F(X,Y) = p_*F_C(X,Y)$,
where $p : \tilde{L} \to L$ is the natural quotient map.

Given a formal group law $F(X,Y)$ over a ring $R$, write
$F(X,Y) = \sum_{i,j \ge 0} a_{i,j} X^i Y^j$. Then there exists a unique ring homomorphism
$\phi : L \to R$ such that $\phi(C_{i,j}) = a_{i,j}$ and $\phi_* F_C(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$.
The uniqueness follows from the condition $\phi_* F_C(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$.

However, the structure of the ring $L$ and the formal group law $F(X,Y)$ constructed
above is not immediately clear. In fact, one can prove that $L$ is isomorphic to
$\ZZ[U] := \ZZ[U_2, U_3, \ldots]$, where $U_i$ are indeterminates, and that
$F(X,Y) \in \ZZ[U][\![X,Y]\!]$ is of the form $f^{-1}(f(X) + f(Y))$ for some
$f(X) \in \QQ[U][\![X]\!]$. The integrality of the coefficients of $F(X,Y)$ then follows
from the functional equation lemma.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}
There is a functor from the category of commutative rings to $\mathbf{Set}$, which assigns
to each commutative ring $R$ the set of formal group laws over $R$, and assigns to each ring
homomorphism $\phi : R \to S$ the pushforward map from formal group laws over $R$ to formal
 group laws over $S$, as defined in Definition~\ref{defn: pushforward of coeff ring}. This is a covariant functor and is corepresented by the ring $L$ and the formal group law $F_C(X,Y)$. More precisely, there is a bijection between $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbf{Ring}}(L, R)$ and the set of formal group laws over $R$.
\end{remark}
