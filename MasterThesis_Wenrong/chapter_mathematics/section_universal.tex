\section{Universal formal group laws}

In this section, we will discuss the construction of universal formal group law and the applications of universal formal group laws.

\begin{definition}
    A one dimensional formal group law $F(X,Y)$ over $L$ is called a universal formal group law iff for any formal group law $G(X,Y)$ over $R$, there is a unique ring homomorphism $\phi : L \to R$, such that $\phi_*F(X,Y) = G(X,Y)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
    The ring $L$ is uniquely determined up to isomorphism. Indeed, let $F(X,Y)$ and $F'(X,Y)$ is
    universal formal group law over ring $L$ and $L'$ respectively. Then there is a ring
    homomorphism $\phi : L \to L'$ and $\psi : L' \to L$ such that $\phi_* F(X,Y) = F'(X,Y)$ and
    $\psi_*F'(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$, then by uniqueness,
    we can deduce that $\phi \psi = \id_{L'}$ and $\psi \phi= \id_L$. \par
    We can show that there exists a universal formal group law over some ring $L$. Let
    $\tilde{L} = \ZZ [C_{i,j}] $, where $C_{i,j}$ is intermediate and $i,j = 1, 2, \ldots$.
    Consider the power series $F_C(X,Y)=X+Y + \sum_{i,j}C_{i,j} X ^i Y ^ j$. And we write
    \begin{equation*}
        F_C(F_C(X,Y),Z) - F_C(X,F_C(Y,Z)) = \sum_{i,j,k} P_{i,j,k}(C) X ^ i Y^j Z^k
    \end{equation*}
    where $P_{i,j,k}(C)$ is some polynomial with variables $C_{i,j}$. Let $I$ be the ideal of
    $\tilde{L}$ generated by $C_{i,j} - C_{j,i}$ and $P_{i,j,k}(C)$. Let $L = \tilde{L}/ I$ and
    $F(X,Y) = p_*F_C(X,Y)$, where $p : \tilde{L} \to L$ be natural quotient map. Therefore,
    given a formal group law $F(X,Y)$ over ring $R$, if we denote $F(X,Y) = a_{ij} X ^ i Y ^ j$,
    then there is a unique ring homomorphism from $\phi: L \to R$ such that
    $\phi(C_{ij}) = a_{ij}$ and $\phi_* F_C(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$. The uniqueness is given by the equation
    $\phi_* F_C(X,Y) = F(X,Y)$.

    However, we know nothing about the structure of ring $L$ and $F(X,Y)$ above. In fact, we could
    prove that $L$ is isomorphic to $\ZZ[U]$ and $F(X,Y) \in \ZZ [U][\![X]\!] $ is of the form
    $f^{-1}(f(X) + f(Y))$ for some $f(X) \in \QQ[U][\![X]\!] $. And the integrality of the
    coefficients of $F(X,Y)$ follows by the functional equation lemma.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}
    There is a functor from commutative ring category to type, which map commutative ring $R$ to
    formal group over $R$ and ring homomorphism $\phi : R \to S$ to pushforward map from formal
    groups over $R$ to formal groups over $S$ which is defined in definition
    $\ref{defn: pushforward of coeff ring}$. This is a covariant functor and is corepresented by
    ring $L$ and $F_C(X,Y)$. To be specific, there is a bijection between
    $\mathcal{H}om_{Ring}(L, R)$ and formal group laws over $R$.
\end{remark}
